⚠️ CRITICAL: The $9.99 Threshold Changes Everything. Going from $8.99 → $9.99 doesn't just add $1 to price — it jumps your royalty rate from 50% to 60%. At $8.99 you earn $2.30/sale. At $9.99 you earn $3.79/sale — that's 65% more per sale for just $1 more in price. NEVER price a paperback at $7.99 or $8.99 unless you absolutely must.
Strategic Implication: Design ALL low-content books to justify $9.99+. Add enough pages (100–120 minimum), include useful extras (pace charts, checklists, reference pages), and use quality branding. This pushes you into 60% royalty territory. For medium-content and ebooks, price at $12.99+ for maximum margin.
Break-Even ACoS matters for ads. At $9.99 paperback ($3.79 royalty), your break-even ACoS is ~38%. This means for every $1 you spend on ads, you need at least $2.63 in book sales revenue. At $7.99 ($1.80 royalty), break-even ACoS drops to just 22% — almost impossible to run profitable ads. Ebooks are the ad-friendliest format with 69% break-even ACoS.

Competitor Weakness Analysis — What Buyers Complain About

Synthesized from 1–3 star reviews, running community forums, and product review articles across your target niches. Each complaint = a feature you should include in YOUR book.

🏃 Running Log / Marathon Training Journals — Top Complaints

#Buyer ComplaintWhat They Actually WantYour Product Fix
1"Just basic lined pages with distance and time — nothing useful"Structured training-specific fieldsInclude pace, splits, heart rate, effort level (RPE), shoe worn, terrain, weather, elevation
2"No actual training plan included"A progressive weekly schedule they can followInclude a 12–16 week progressive training plan with easy/hard days mapped out
3"No nutrition or fueling tracking"Pre-run/post-run nutrition loggingAdd pre-run meal, during-run fueling, hydration tracking, and post-run recovery nutrition fields
4"Doesn't track shoe mileage"Running shoe rotation and mileage logInclude a shoe log page (shoe name, purchase date, cumulative miles — replace at 350–500 mi)
5"No race-day preparation section"A race-day checklist and strategy plannerAdd race-day planner: goal time, pacing strategy, nutrition plan, gear checklist, warm-up routine
6"Too short — only 12 weeks"Longer training cycles for marathon prepOffer both 12-week (half) and 16–24-week (full marathon) versions
7"No pace charts or conversion tables"Quick-reference pace per mile/km tablesInclude pace charts, min/mile to min/km conversion, and goal-time pacing guides
8"Paper quality is too thin"Paper that doesn't bleed throughUse cream/white 60lb paper (KDP default is fine), mention "thick quality paper" in listing
9"No mental/mindset tracking"How they FELT during the run, not just statsAdd "How I Felt" field with effort scale (1–10), mood, mental notes, and motivation level
10"No cross-training or rest day sections"Track strength, yoga, rest alongside runningInclude cross-training log and designated rest day reflection pages

💼 Business Planners — Top Complaints
