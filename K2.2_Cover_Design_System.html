KDP Cover Technical Specifications

Your cover is a single PDF file containing the back cover + spine + front cover as one continuous image. Get any dimension wrong and KDP will reject your upload instantly.
SpecificationRequirementWhy It Matters
File formatPDF only (for print cover)KDP does not accept JPG/PNG for paperback covers. Canva exports PDF natively.
Resolution300 DPI minimumBelow 300 DPI = blurry print = rejection. Canva PDF Print export handles this automatically.
Color modeCMYK recommended, RGB acceptedKDP converts RGB to CMYK automatically. Colors may shift slightly. Canva uses RGB — that's fine.
Bleed0.125" (3.2mm) on ALL sidesBleed = extra image beyond trim line. Without it, you get white edges after cutting. Required if any color/image touches the edge.
Safe zone0.25" (6.4mm) from outside edgeKeep ALL text and important content inside this zone. Anything closer to the edge may be cut off.
Spine textOnly allowed on books with 79+ pagesAll 8 of your books qualify (100+ pages each). Keep spine text centered with 0.0625" (1.6mm) padding on each side.
BarcodeKDP adds automatically to back coverLeave a blank white rectangle ~2" × 1.2" in the bottom-right of back cover for the auto-barcode. Don't design your own.
File sizeUnder 650MBCanva exports are typically 5-15MB. No issue here.
eBook coverSeparate JPEG, 2560 × 1600pxFront cover only. Upload as a separate file during KDP listing. Same design as your paperback front cover, just different dimensions.
The #1 rejection reason: Wrong cover dimensions. Your total cover width depends on page count (which determines spine width). ALWAYS use the KDP Cover Calculator at kdp.amazon.com/cover-calculator to get exact dimensions for each book. Enter: Paperback → B&W → White paper → your trim size → your page count → download the template PNG.

DESIGN

3 Niche Cover Design Systems

Each pen name gets a distinct visual identity. Buyers should recognize books from the same "publisher" at a glance. Here are your three brand systems:

🏠 Niche A: Dad & Family (Books 1, 4, 7)

Vibe: Warm, heartfelt, approachable. Think handwritten notes on a kitchen table. These are gift books — the cover must say "buy this for someone you love."
ElementSpecification
Primary color Navy Blue #1E3A5F — trust, fatherhood, classic
Accent color Warm Amber #F4A261 — warmth, sunshine, golden hour
Background Cream #FAF5EF — soft, warm, inviting. Never pure white.
Text contrast Deep Ink #1A1A2E — dark navy/near-black for readability
Title fontPlayfair Display Bold — elegant serif with personality. Feels classic and meaningful.
Subtitle fontMontserrat Regular — clean sans-serif for contrast. Reads well at small sizes.
Decorative elementsSimple line illustrations: baby footprints, heart outline, stars, small icons. Nothing photographic — illustration only. Canva "Line Art" style.
Layout patternTitle top-third (large), subtle illustration center, subtitle + pen name bottom. Lots of breathing room. Feels premium, not cluttered.
Matte vs GlossyMatte finish — feels premium for gift books. KDP lets you choose at upload.

💼 Niche B: Business & E-Commerce (Books 2, 6, 8)

Vibe: Bold, professional, action-oriented. These are tools for doers. Cover should scream "this will make you money."
ElementSpecification
Primary color Dark Navy #0F172A — authority, professionalism
Accent color Emerald Green #10B981 — money, growth, go
Secondary accent Gold #F59E0B — premium, achievement, success
Background White #FFFFFF or Dark Navy #0F172A — high contrast either way
Title fontOswald Bold — strong, modern, commanding. ALL CAPS for titles.
Subtitle fontOpen Sans Regular — clean, professional, readable.
Decorative elementsGeometric shapes: clean lines, arrows, grid patterns, subtle circuit/tech patterns. Bold color blocks. No illustrations — pure graphic design.
Layout patternBold color block at top (60%) with title, clean bottom section (40%) with subtitle. Or full dark background with centered large title. Sharp, structured, no curves.
Matte vs GlossyGlossy finish — looks polished and professional on bookshelf. Pops in Amazon photos.

🏃 Niche C: Running & Fitness (Books 3, 5)

Vibe: Energetic, aspirational, outdoorsy. Cover should make you want to lace up and go run.
ElementSpecification
Primary color Runner Red #DC2626 — energy, passion, race-day intensity
Accent color Charcoal #1E293B — grounded, serious athlete
Secondary accent Orange #F97316 — sunrise, momentum, warmth
Background White #FFFFFF — clean, sporty, energetic
Title fontBebas Neue — tall, athletic, bold. All caps. Sporty without being childish.
Subtitle fontLato Regular — friendly, clean, modern.
Decorative elementsRoad/path lines, subtle topographic patterns, running shoe silhouettes, distance markers, finish line stripes. Dynamic diagonals — nothing static.
Layout patternDiagonal color split (red/white at an angle), title large and bold in the dominant zone. Energy feels built into the layout through asymmetry. Runner silhouette or road graphic optional.
Matte vs GlossyMatte finish — grippier feel for a fitness book. Feels practical and tactile.
RULES

7 Cover Design Rules for Low-Content Books

#RuleWhyHow
1Readable at thumbnail size90% of Amazon shoppers see your cover as a tiny image on a search results page. If they can't read the title, they scroll past.Title text must fill at least 40% of the cover width. View your design at 150px wide before exporting — can you read every word?
2Title is the heroLow-content books sell on clarity, not mystery. The buyer needs to know EXACTLY what this book is in 1 second.Title is the largest text element. Center it or place top-third. Never let decorative elements compete with the title for attention.
3Max 3 colorsMore colors = visual noise = amateur look. Professional covers use restraint.Primary + accent + neutral (white/cream/black). That's it. Your niche color system already defines these.
4Never use stock photosStock photos on low-content book covers look cheap and generic. They signal "template." Abstract graphics, patterns, and illustrations look premium.Use Canva's built-in shapes, lines, and graphic elements. Flat color blocks with clean typography always outperform stock imagery.
5Subtitle sells the productTitle names the book. Subtitle explains WHY to buy it. Timeframe + audience + benefit."A First-Time Father's Memory Book & Milestone Tracker for Baby's First 12 Months" — that subtitle alone could sell the book.
6Consistent pen name stylingAuthor name should be small, subtle, bottom of cover. Pen name builds brand across books in the same niche.Author name in subtitle font at ~40% of title size. Same position on every cover in the niche series. Bottom-center or bottom-right.
7Spine matches front coverBooks displayed spine-out on physical shelves (and in "Look Inside" views) need a readable, branded spine.Title + pen name on spine. Same fonts as front cover. Background matches front cover primary color. Text runs bottom-to-top.

WORKFLOW

Canva Cover Design Workflow — Step by Step

Complete workflow from blank canvas to upload-ready PDF. Do the one-time setup first, then each cover takes 30–45 minutes.
ONE-TIME SETUP (do once, use forever)
StepActionTime
1Create a Canva account (free tier works). Upload your brand fonts if not already available: Playfair Display, Montserrat, Oswald, Open Sans, Bebas Neue, Lato. All are free on Google Fonts.10 min
2Save your color palettes as a text note or Canva Brand Kit (free tier allows 1 palette): Dad niche (navy, amber, cream), Business (dark navy, emerald, gold), Running (red, charcoal, orange).5 min
3Download KDP cover templates for all 8 books from kdp.amazon.com/cover-calculator. Save the PNG templates to a "KDP Templates" folder.15 min
PER-BOOK COVER DESIGN (30–45 min each)
StepActionTime
1New Custom Size in Canva. Enter the exact Full Cover Width × Full Cover Height from the spine table above (e.g., 12.520" × 9.250" for Book 1). Set to inches. 300 DPI is automatic for PDF export.30 sec
2Upload and place KDP template. Upload the KDP template PNG as a background image. Stretch to fill. This shows your fold lines, spine position, barcode zone, and safe areas. You'll design ON TOP of this template, then delete it before export.1 min
3Set up background color. Draw a rectangle covering the full canvas in your niche's background color. Place it behind the template (which should be semi-transparent or on a layer above).1 min
4Design the front cover (right side of the spread). Place title text, subtitle, pen name, and decorative elements following your niche's blueprint from the section above. Stay inside the safe zone lines shown on the template.15 min
5Design the spine. Rotate text 90° (bottom to top). Title in niche title font. Pen name at bottom. Match front cover's primary background color. Keep text centered within spine boundaries.5 min
6Design the back cover (left side of spread). Use the back cover template from the section above. Leave barcode zone empty (bottom-right of back cover = bottom-LEFT of the full spread).10 min
7Delete the KDP template overlay. Click on the template PNG and delete it. Your design should now be a clean, full cover with no template marks visible.15 sec
8Thumbnail test. Zoom out until the front cover portion is roughly the size of a thumbnail on your screen. Can you read the title? Does the design pop? If not, simplify or enlarge the title.30 sec
9Export as PDF Print. Canva → Share → Download → PDF Print (highest quality). This auto-sets 300 DPI and embeds fonts. Save as "Book[#]_Cover_v1.pdf".30 sec
10Create eBook cover. Duplicate the Canva file. Crop to front cover only. Resize to 2560 × 1600px. Export as JPEG (highest quality). Save as "Book[#]_eBook_Cover.jpg".3 min
Total time for all 8 covers: One-time setup (30 min) + 8 covers × 40 min each = ~6 hours total. Spread across 2 sessions. Do all 3 Dad covers in one session, then Business, then Running — staying in one brand system keeps you faster and more consistent.
AVOID

8 Common KDP Cover Rejection Reasons

#Rejection ReasonHow to Prevent It
1Wrong dimensionsAlways use KDP Cover Calculator. Verify full cover width matches exactly. If page count changes, recalculate everything.
2Missing bleedExtend all background colors/images 0.125" beyond trim lines on all sides. If using Canva custom size with exact dimensions from calculator, this is built in.
3Text outside safe zoneKeep all text 0.25" inside the trim line. Upload the KDP template PNG as a guide layer.
4Spine text on thin booksOnly add spine text if 79+ pages. All your books qualify, but if you reduce page count, check this.
5Low resolution imagesCanva PDF Print export = 300 DPI automatically. Don't use low-res PNGs or JPGs from the internet as decorative elements.
6Cover references pricingNever put "$12.99" or "FREE" or pricing language on your cover. KDP terms prohibit this.
7Misleading content claimsDon't put "#1 Bestseller" or "Award Winner" on the cover unless it's genuinely true. KDP flags this.
8Barcode area obstructedLeave a 2" × 1.2" blank white zone in the bottom-right of your back cover. Always.
