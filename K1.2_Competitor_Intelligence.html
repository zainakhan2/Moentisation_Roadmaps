NICHE #1

👨‍👧‍👦 Dad Journals & Father Keepsakes

Market dominated by 2–3 quality players (Andrew Shaw, Family Memory Books, Hear Your Story) and hundreds of low-effort KDP knock-offs. The gap between the top and bottom is enormous — and you can position right in the quality tier at a competitive price.

1. "First-Time Dad Journal" — Andrew Shaw MARKET LEADER

📊 BSR ~40K–80K⭐ 4.5+📝 100+ reviews💰 $14.99📄 ~160pg📦 Callisto Media
Strengths: Real parenting columnist with media credentials. Monthly check-ins from trimester 1 through baby's first months. Combines guided prompts with to-do lists. Professional publisher = quality design/editing. Expert endorsements. Multi-retailer distribution.
Focuses only on pregnancy → early months. No toddler years. No standalone practical tools (checklists, trackers). At $14.99, there's room for a $12.99 competitor. Prompts are polished but feel "edited by committee" — lacks raw authenticity.
Extend coverage through Year 1 into toddler territory. Include practical standalone pages (hospital bag, pediatrician log, baby gear checklist). Price at $12.99. Write with a real dad's raw, honest voice — not a columnist's polished tone. Your lived experience IS the differentiator.

2. "Dad Pregnancy Journal" — Family Memory Books STRONG

📊 BSR ~30K–70K⭐ 4.4📝 200+ reviews💰 $9.99📄 88pg📦 KDP
Strengths: 41-week calendar with baby development descriptions. Guided prompts. Sections: pregnancy, birth, about dad, life lessons. Gender-neutral cover. Photo pages. Strong review count for KDP product.
Only 88 pages — feels thin for a keepsake. Ends at birth. No practical sections. Prompts are functional but generic. Reviews mention wanting more writing space per prompt. No post-birth content.
Double the page count (150+). Include post-birth Year 1 milestones. More writing space per prompt (full page, not half). Add practical checklists they're missing.

3. "Dad, I Want to Hear Your Story" — Hear Your Story #1 BEST SELLER

📊 BSR ~5K–15K⭐ 4.7📝 15,000+ reviews💰 $13.99📄 120pg📦 21-book series
Strengths: Massively successful brand. Part of 21-book series. Hardcover + spiral options. Prompts help dads share LIFE STORY. 15K+ reviews = insurmountable social proof. Multiple covers and formats.
NOT actually a new-dad/pregnancy journal — targets older kids giving dad a journal to share his life story. Dominates "dad journal" keywords but serves a different purpose entirely. No pregnancy/baby content.
Don't compete head-on. LEARN from the series model — build YOUR dad journals into a 3–4 book series. Use specific long-tail keywords ("new dad pregnancy journal") to avoid their keyword dominance. Potentially create a "Letters to My Baby" book as your equivalent to their "Hear Your Story" approach.

4. "Dad's Letters to Baby" + "Pregnancy Journal for Dad-To-Be" (Various) MID-TIER

📊 BSR ~50K–200K⭐ 4.0–4.4📝 20–100 reviews💰 $8.99–$11.99📄 80–120pg
Strengths: Letter-based formats (47 prompts from dad to child). Emotional keepsake positioning. Clean modern designs. Affordable pricing.
Letter format only — no practical sections. No week-by-week tracking. Limited space per letter. Not comprehensive standalone products. Covers often look feminine/pink despite targeting dads.

5. KDP Low-Quality Knock-offs (70%+ of search results) WEAK

📊 BSR 500K+⭐ 0–3📝 0–5 reviews💰 $5.99–$7.99📄 50–80pg
Weaknesses: AI-generated covers that look generic. Copy-paste prompts. Thin pages. No author identity. Poor formatting. Pink/pastel covers on "dad" products. Zero marketing. These make up 70%+ of results — they LOWER buyer expectations, meaning a quality product stands out immediately.
Simply being professional puts you in the top 20%. Masculine/premium cover design (dark colors, clean typography). Real author perspective. Quality content. Proper pricing ($12.99 not $5.99).

Your Unique Value Proposition — Dad Journal

"The first dad journal written by a dad who's actually in the trenches." Unlike polished columnist journals or generic KDP knock-offs, yours combines raw authenticity (you're a dad of toddlers RIGHT NOW), practical tools (checklists, trackers, emergency contacts), guided prompts that address real dad experiences (sleep deprivation humor, dad guilt, work-life chaos), and comprehensive coverage from pregnancy through toddler years. Positioned at $12.99 — premium quality, competitive price.

NICHE #2

💼 E-Commerce & Online Business Planners

This market is fragmented — no single dominant player. Most existing products are generic "small business planners" relabeled, or extremely basic KDP journals with "e-commerce" in the title. The gap between what exists and what online sellers ACTUALLY need is massive.

1. "Business Planner for Boutique Owners" — Multiple Covers TOP IN CATEGORY

📊 BSR ~100K–300K⭐ 4.2📝 50+ reviews💰 $6.99–$8.99📄 ~100pg📦 KDP
Strengths: Specifically targets boutique owners. Multiple cover variants for different aesthetics. Includes: supplies, shipping, inventory, sales, expenses, income, tax deductions, goals, marketing planning. Addresses a real audience (boutique/fashion resellers).
Priced too low ($6.99) — earning barely $1 per sale. Interior design is basic. No digital marketing/social media sections. No platform-specific tracking (Shopify, Etsy, Amazon). No product sourcing or supplier management. Feels like a generic ledger with "boutique" on the cover.
Price at $12.99 with genuinely useful e-commerce specific interiors. Include: supplier contact database, product sourcing tracker, inventory by SKU, platform-specific analytics pages, social media content calendar, advertising spend tracker, profit margin calculator templates. Your e-commerce + fashion retail experience means you know what sections actually matter.

2. "The Best E-Commerce Planner" — Symphony Girl Designs WEAK EXECUTION

📊 BSR 1M+⭐ Limited📝 Few reviews💰 $12.99📄 ~100pg📦 KDP
Strengths: Attempts to be e-commerce specific. Undated (reusable). Year-long planning scope.
BSR over 1M means essentially no sales. Poor cover design. Interior is generic time management, not e-commerce operational. Title is overly bold for the actual content quality. No reviews suggesting no marketing effort.
The high price ($12.99) with no sales proves that price alone doesn't sell — you need quality + marketing. Your book needs genuinely valuable interiors AND a launch strategy with KDP ads and community promotion.

3. "E-Commerce Planner" — S.D. (basic KDP) MINIMAL

📊 BSR 2M+📝 0 reviews💰 $8.99📄 ~80pg📦 KDP
Described features: Shop/account setup, SEO planning, single product planning. Positioned for Woocommerce, Etsy, Wix, Amazon sellers.
Zero sales traction. Too thin (80 pages). No reviews. Unclear what the interior actually looks like. No author credibility established.

4. Etsy Digital Planners (2,000+ results) INDIRECT COMPETITION

📊 N/A (Etsy)⭐ Varies💰 $3.99–$14.99 (digital)📦 Digital download / Canva template
Important context: Over 2,000 "ecommerce planner" products on Etsy — but these are DIGITAL products (PDFs, Canva templates). This proves demand exists. People ARE searching for e-commerce planning tools. But there's a segment that prefers PHYSICAL planners (writing by hand, carrying to meetings, having on desk). KDP paperback serves this segment.
The Etsy digital planner market validates demand. Your KDP paperback serves the physical-product segment. Mention "prefer pen and paper?" in your Amazon listing to capture buyers who considered digital but want physical.

5. Generic "Small Business Planner" books (thousands) SATURATED BUT GENERIC

📊 BSR varies widely💰 $6.99–$14.99📄 80–200pg
Pattern: Thousands of "small business planner" books exist. Top sellers have strong BSR. But they're generic — they work for a bakery, a lawn service, or an online store equally. None are specifically designed for e-commerce operational needs like inventory management, order tracking, multi-platform selling, or digital marketing analytics.
Generic = no competitive advantage. A bakery owner and a Shopify seller have COMPLETELY different planning needs. Nobody is specifically serving e-commerce sellers with tailored operational tools in physical book format.
Own the "e-commerce planner" keyword space by being the ONLY quality physical planner specifically designed for online sellers. Include platform-specific sections (Shopify dashboard tracking, Etsy stats, Amazon seller metrics). This specificity is your moat.

Your Unique Value Proposition — E-Commerce Planner

"The only business planner built by someone who's actually run an online store." Unlike generic small business planners, yours includes: SKU-level inventory tracking, supplier management database, platform-specific analytics pages (Shopify/Etsy/Amazon), order fulfillment log, shipping cost tracker, social media content calendar, ad spend & ROAS tracker, and seasonal buying calendar for fashion retail. Built from real operational experience — not a generic template with "e-commerce" slapped on the cover.

NICHE #3

🏃 Running Logs & Marathon Training Journals

Market has one dominant traditional publisher (Runner's World) and hundreds of low-quality KDP products. The BSR data shows a bifurcation: quality products with real training content sell well; generic "running log" notebooks do not sell at all.

1. "Runner's World Training Journal" ESTABLISHED LEADER

📊 BSR ~15K–30K⭐ 4.5+📝 800+ reviews💰 $14.99📄 192pg📦 Traditional publisher (Rodale)
Strengths: Backed by the Runner's World brand. Full-color photos. Motivation tips. Pace charts for 200m to marathon. Year's worth of run tracking. Cross-training sections. Stretching and recovery info. Nutrition tips embedded. Weekly motivational quotes. Well-known in running community.
Full-color = more expensive to produce on KDP. Brand power is unbeatable in general running. NOT specifically a half marathon or marathon training plan — it's a general running log. Published years ago, hasn't been meaningfully updated. Somewhat overwhelming for beginners. No race-day specific preparation tools.
Don't try to out-general Runner's World. Go SPECIFIC: "Half Marathon Training Log" or "First Marathon Planner." They own "running journal" — you own "half marathon training plan + tracker." Include what they don't: progressive week-by-week training schedule, race-day checklist, nutrition timing guide, shoe mileage tracker.

2. "Marathon & Half-Marathon Training Journal" — Dariusz Janczewski NICHE AUTHORITY

📊 BSR ~80K–200K⭐ 4.3📝 50+ reviews💰 $14.95📄 250pg📦 Traditional pub
Strengths: Written by a real competitive runner (2:42 Boston Marathon). Comprehensive: 53 weekly spreads, racing calendar, pacing charts, stretching guide, nutrition planning, contacts. Designed to last a full year. Includes weightlifting log for cross-training.
Published in 2010 — very outdated. Design feels dated. 250 pages is heavy/thick. Calendars are from 2010/2011 (!). No modern training concepts (heart rate zones, perceived effort scales). Not beginner-friendly. Price high at $14.95.
A MODERN version of this concept with: current training science, undated format (reusable), cleaner design, beginner-friendly language, and specific half-marathon focus. This is essentially your blueprint — but updated for 2026 runners.

3. "First Marathon Training Journal" — MOKA Journals DIRECT COMPETITOR

📊 BSR ~200K–500K⭐ 4.0📝 10–30 reviews💰 $9.99📄 ~150pg📦 KDP
Strengths: Specifically targets first-time marathon runners. 24-week training plan in miles AND kilometers. Daily running logs. Training tips on mental and physical aspects. Race-day planning section. Motivational quotes. Milestone race planning section. Available at accessible $9.99 price.
24 weeks is unusually long for most training plans (16–20 is standard). Interior design is basic. Limited nutrition tracking. No shoe mileage log. No heart rate or effort tracking. Motivational quotes take up space that could be functional. Cover designs are simple and generic.
Your version: 16-week plan (standard for half marathon), include pace/splits tracking, effort scale (RPE), shoe log, pre-run nutrition, weather conditions, cross-training log, and a race-day strategy planner. Make the interior genuinely useful to a runner, not just a template with running words on it.

4–5. Generic "Running Log Book 2026" Products (hundreds) DEAD ON ARRIVAL

📊 BSR 1M–5M+⭐ 0–2📝 0 reviews💰 $6.99–$8.99📄 80–110pg
Pattern: Hundreds of near-identical "Running Log Book 2026" products with: generic covers, basic distance/time/pace fields, no training plans, no nutritional guidance, no race preparation. BSRs above 1M confirm zero sales. These products validate that SIMPLY HAVING a running log on Amazon doesn't generate sales.
No differentiation. No training structure. No author credibility. Dated titles (2026 edition) become worthless in 12 months. Priced below $9.99 = 50% royalty tier = barely profitable even if they sold.
Go UNDATED (evergreen, sells for years). Include a real training PLAN, not just empty logging pages. Establish authority in your listing: "Designed by a marathon runner." Price at $9.99 for 60% royalty. Invest in a professional cover that signals quality.

Your Unique Value Proposition — Running Log

"A training PLAN, not just a log book — designed by an actual marathon runner." Unlike generic running logs (which don't sell), yours includes a structured 16-week progressive training schedule for half-marathon, pace charts and goal-time calculators, nutrition timing for long runs, shoe rotation and mileage tracker, race-day strategy planner with checklist, mental game notes and RPE tracking, and cross-training integration. Undated so it sells year-round. Priced at $9.99. Your running experience makes every page credible.
