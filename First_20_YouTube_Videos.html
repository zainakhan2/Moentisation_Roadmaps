The First South Asian Victoria Cross — Khudadad Khan at Ypres 1914
A wounded machine gunner, alone, every comrade dead — the 26-year-old who fired until there was no one left
SOUTH ASIAN 4.5h production Week 8, Thursday 3 Shorts
▶
On October 31, 1914, at Hollebeke, Belgium, Sepoy Khudadad Khan's entire machine gun team was dead. Every man killed by German bullets or bayonets. Khan himself was wounded. But he kept firing — alone — holding off the German advance long enough for reinforcements to arrive. He was left for dead. He crawled back to his regiment in the darkness. He was 26 years old. And he became the first South Asian in history to earn the Victoria Cross.
Why This Topic
The first South Asian VC winner is a story almost nobody knows — even in South Asia. Connects powerfully to V1 (Indian Army WW2), V2 (Imphal), and V9 (Gurkhas). The fact that Indian soldiers weren't even ELIGIBLE for the VC until 1911 — just 3 years before Khudadad Khan earned one — adds a powerful colonial injustice angle that resonates with your channel thesis. Khan was from what is now Pakistan, adding cross-border appeal. The story spans WW1 (not WW2), diversifying your timeline. King George V personally visited his hospital bed to award it — incredible visual moment.
Script Structure
Act 1: The injustice — Indian soldiers fought for Britain for decades but were DENIED the VC until 1911. The Indian Order of Merit was considered "good enough for natives." The campaign to change this. Why it matters. Act 2: Khudadad Khan's story — born in Punjab (now Pakistan), 129th Baluchis, sent to Western Front October 1914, First Battle of Ypres. The German assault at Hollebeke. His entire team killed. Wounded, alone, firing until the last possible moment. Left for dead, crawling back through no-man's land. Act 3: The wider story — 11 VCs to Indian soldiers in WW1, 28 in WW2. Darwan Singh Negi (second Indian VC, same month). The 153 total VCs to Indian Army. Khan's later life — retired as Subedar, met Queen Elizabeth II in 1956, died 1971 in Pakistan. His statue stands at Pakistan Army Museum. The medal made from captured Russian cannons. Legacy: Abdul Hafiz VC at Imphal (connects V2), Lachhiman Gurung VC (connects V9).
Key Facts
Sepoy Khudadad Khan, 129th Duke of Connaught's Own Baluchis, October 31 1914, Hollebeke Belgium. First Battle of Ypres. All 5 comrades killed. Khan wounded but kept firing alone. Crawled back in darkness. Awarded VC by King George V in person at Indian Hospital, Brighton Pavilion, January 26 1915. Indian soldiers only eligible for VC from 1911. Total 153 VCs to Indian Army. 11 in WW1, 28 in WW2. Khan retired as Subedar, died 1971 Pakistan.
Thumbnail Concept
Dramatic archival photo of Indian soldier with turban and machine gun on Western Front. Text: THE FIRST SOUTH ASIAN VICTORIA CROSS. Red badge: 1914. Dark overlay with amber accent bar. The turban on the Western Front is an inherently striking and unusual image.
