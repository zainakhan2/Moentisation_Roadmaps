SPECS

2026 Instagram Format Specifications

Instagram shifted to a taller 3:4 grid preview in late 2025. Your content must be designed for this new vertical format while staying readable in the feed. Here are the exact dimensions:
FormatDimensionsAspect RatioNotes
Feed posts1080 × 1350 px4:5The standard for feed posts and carousels. Taller than old 1:1 square — more screen real estate.
Grid previewCropped to 3:43:4The grid now shows a taller preview. Keep all key text/visuals in the center square area for safety.
Reels1080 × 1920 px9:16Full vertical screen. Same as YouTube Shorts — your cross-posts are already the right size.
Reel covers1080 × 1920 px9:16 (designed), 3:4 (grid crop)Design at 9:16 but keep title text centered in the middle 1080×1350 area — that's what the grid shows.
Stories1080 × 1920 px9:16Leave 250px free at top and bottom for Instagram's UI overlays (username bar, reply bar).
Carousel slides1080 × 1350 px4:5All slides same size. Vertical format dominates screen in feed. Max 20 slides per carousel.
Highlight covers1080 × 1920 px9:16 (only center circle visible)Design icon in center — Instagram crops to a small circle for the highlight preview.
The safe zone rule: Instagram's new 3:4 grid crops the top and bottom of your 4:5 posts. Always keep titles, faces, and key visuals in the CENTER of your image. If it's readable in a 1:1 square crop, it'll work in both the old and new grid formats.

Your YouTube brand from Y1.3 translates directly to Instagram. These 6 colors define every visual on your page:

The same reason dark YouTube thumbnails outperform light ones applies to Instagram: contrast = attention.

TEMPLATES

4 Canva Templates — Build Once, Reuse Forever

Create these 4 templates in Canva. Every Instagram post you ever make will be a duplicate-and-edit of one of these. Total setup time: ~45 minutes.

Template 1: Reel Cover (for all cross-posted YouTube Shorts)

SpecDetails
Canva size1080 × 1920 px (9:16)
BackgroundBlackout (#0A0A0A) full canvas
LayoutTitle text centered in the MIDDLE 1080×1350 zone (this is the grid-visible area). Above and below that zone are "overflow" areas only visible in the Reels tab.
TitleOswald Bold, Dispatch White (#FAFAF9), ALL CAPS, 2-4 words maximum. Font size: 72-96pt. Centered horizontally and vertically within the safe zone.
BadgeTop area: red (#DC2626) rectangle badge with "UNTOLD" / "FORGOTTEN" / "CLASSIFIED" in white, same as YouTube thumbnail badges.
Accent barAmber (#D97706) horizontal line, 4px thick, below the title text. Spans ~60% of width. Centered.
SubtitleOptional: Libre Baskerville Italic, Foxhole Grey (#78716C), small text below the accent bar. Video topic context.
Bottom branding"THE FORGOTTEN FRONT" in Special Elite, amber (#D97706), 20% opacity, bottom of safe zone. Subtle watermark.
This is essentially your YouTube thumbnail adapted for vertical format. Same fonts, same colors, same badges. A viewer who sees your YouTube thumbnail AND your Instagram Reel cover should instantly know they're from the same channel. Brand consistency across platforms is how you build recognition.

Template 2: Carousel Slides (for "5 Things You Didn't Know" deep dives)

SpecDetails
Canva size1080 × 1350 px (4:5) — all slides same size
Slide 1 (Hook)Blackout background. Oswald Bold title, white, ALL CAPS: "5 THINGS YOU DIDN'T KNOW ABOUT [TOPIC]". Amber accent bar below. Red badge "UNTOLD" top-left. This slide must stop the scroll — maximum contrast, maximum text size.
Slides 2-6 (Facts)Bunker (#1C1917) background. Numbered fact (Oswald Bold, amber, large: "01"). Fact text (Libre Baskerville, white, 24-28pt). Archival image as background with 70% dark overlay — or no image, pure typography on dark background. One fact per slide.
Slide 7 (Summary)Blackout background. "DID YOU KNOW ALL 5?" in Oswald Bold, amber. Brief recap or "Which one surprised you most?" engagement prompt.
Slide 8 (CTA)Blackout background. "FOLLOW @theforgottenfront" in Oswald Bold, white. "For the stories they never taught you." in Libre Baskerville Italic, grey. Your logo centered above.
ConsistencyEvery slide has amber accent bar at bottom (4px, full width). Every slide has "THE FORGOTTEN FRONT" watermark in Special Elite, amber, 15% opacity, bottom-right corner.
Carousel performance in 2026: Carousels generate the highest saves-per-reach ratio of any format. Each swipe = dwell time signal to the algorithm. 5-8 slides is the sweet spot. The CTA slide drives follows from non-followers who discover your carousel via Explore.

GRID

Grid Aesthetic Strategy — The "Dark Archive" Look

Your grid is your storefront. When someone lands on your profile, they scan the top 9 posts in ~3 seconds to decide whether to follow. Here's how to make those 3 seconds count:
PrincipleHow You Apply It
Dark-dominant grid90%+ of your posts have dark backgrounds (#0A0A0A or #1C1917). This creates a striking, cohesive "dark archive" aesthetic that's instantly recognizable and signals "serious history content" to visitors.
Amber pops of colorAmber (#D97706) appears on every post as accent bars, title highlights, or badges. When a visitor sees your grid, they see a field of dark with consistent amber touches — unmistakably branded.
Mix content types in the gridAlternate Reels, carousels, and fact cards. Never post 3 Reels in a row (they all look similar in the grid). The visual variety of Reel covers vs. carousel hooks vs. text cards creates a dynamic, professional grid.
Reel covers are designed, not random framesEvery Reel gets a custom cover image (Template 1) — never a random video frame. Random frames create a messy, amateur grid. Designed covers create a curated, intentional look.
Text readability at grid sizeWhen viewing the grid, each post title should be readable. This means BIG text (2-4 words max on Reel covers and carousel hooks). If you can't read it in the grid, simplify.

PIN

Pinned Posts — Your Top 3 Grid Positions

Instagram lets you pin up to 3 posts to the top of your grid. These are the first things new visitors see. Use them strategically:
Pin PositionWhat to PinWhen to Update
Pin 1 (left)Your best-performing Reel — the one with the highest views/engagement. Let the algorithm's vote of confidence be the first thing visitors see.Monthly — swap to your current top performer
Pin 2 (center)Your best carousel — the one with highest saves. Carousels demonstrate the depth and quality of your content.Monthly — swap to current best
Pin 3 (right)"About" or "Start Here" post — a single image explaining what the channel is about with a CTA to follow. Acts as a static channel trailer.Rarely — only update if your channel focus shifts
Don't pin anything until you have 12+ posts. You need enough content for the grid to look full below the pinned row. Pinning too early with only 3-4 posts makes the profile look empty.
SEO

Alt Text & Visual SEO — Every Post

Instagram's AI reads your images to categorize content. Alt text helps both accessibility AND search discovery. Add it to every post:
WhereHowExample
Alt text on feed postsWhen posting → Advanced settings → Write Alt Text. Describe the image using niche keywords naturally."Archival photograph of Indian soldiers marching through Burma jungle during World War 2, 1944. Military history."
Reel cover textInstagram's AI reads on-screen text in Reels for topic categorization. Your title text on Reel covers serves double duty: visual hook + algorithm keyword signal.Title text "2.5 MILLION FORGOTTEN" tells the algorithm this Reel is about military history and forgotten soldiers.
Reel audioInstagram transcribes your voiceover audio and uses it for content categorization and search. Clear, keyword-rich narration helps discovery.Your military history narration naturally contains keywords the algorithm needs.
Caption keywordsInstagram's search indexes caption text. Use natural language with niche terms in every caption."The Indian Army in WW2 was the largest volunteer force in history — 2.5 million soldiers who fought across Burma, Italy, and North Africa..."
