• Red badge for content type labels

Voice & Narration Strategy

Hybrid (yours + AI)Your voice for main content, AI for Shorts or supplementary.Inconsistency may confuse brand identity.$0–$22/moLATER EXPANSION
Strong recommendation: Use your own voice. Here's why this is strategic, not just practical:

1. Your accent is an ASSET, not a liability. A South Asian narrator covering Indian military history is inherently more authentic and credible than a generic American/British AI voice. It signals "this person has a connection to these stories."

2. AI voice channels are flooding YouTube in 2026. Your real voice immediately differentiates you from thousands of faceless AI channels. Viewers can tell and they prefer real humans.

3. It's free. You save $11–22/month and invest that in other tools or KDP ads.

4. Recording setup: minimal. A decent USB microphone ($20–40 one-time), quiet room, and Audacity (free software) for noise reduction. That's it.
Narration style guide:
ElementGuideline
PaceSlower than conversational. Think documentary narrator, not YouTuber. Pause before key reveals.
ToneSerious but not monotone. Convey weight of the subject. Let emotion through on powerful moments.
Opening hookAlways start with the most shocking or emotional fact. "87,000 soldiers died fighting for an empire that forgot their names."
Story structureHook → Context → Build-up → Climax → Aftermath → Reflection. Classic documentary arc.
Signature sign-off"This was The Forgotten Front. The stories they never taught you." — consistent ending builds brand recognition.
