Step-by-Step: How to Make One Video

This is the exact SOP (Standard Operating Procedure) you'll follow for every long-form video on The Forgotten Front.

Step 1: Research & Topic Selection (30 min)

ActionToolTimeDetails
Choose topic from content calendarYour editorial calendar2 minRefer to your 8 content pillars and first 10 video ideas from Y1.1
Deep researchClaude + Wikipedia + academic sources20 minPrompt Claude: "I'm making a 15-min documentary about [topic]. Give me the key facts, dramatic moments, lesser-known details, and a narrative arc." Then verify facts with Wikipedia citations and primary sources.
Collect visual assetsWikimedia / IWM / LoC8 minDownload 15–25 archival images, maps, and photographs relevant to the topic. Save to a folder named for this video.

Step 2: Scriptwriting (45 min)

ActionToolTimeDetails
Generate AI first draftClaude10 minPrompt: "Write a 2,000-word documentary script for 'The Forgotten Front' channel about [topic]. Structure: shocking hook (first 15 seconds), context, build-up, climax, aftermath, reflection. Tone: serious, documentary, suspenseful. Include specific dates, names, and numbers."
Rewrite and add your voiceGoogle Docs25 minCRITICAL: Never use AI output as-is. Rewrite 40–60% in your own words. Add personal insights, better transitions, your unique perspective (especially for South Asian content). This is what makes your content un-replicable.
Add visual cuesGoogle Docs10 minIn brackets, note what image/footage should appear: [SHOW: Map of Imphal region] [SHOW: Photo of Indian soldiers in Burma]. This becomes your editing guide.
Script formula (AIDA for documentaries):
Hook (0:00–0:30): Most shocking fact or emotional moment. "87,000 soldiers died fighting a war that wasn't theirs..."
Context (0:30–3:00): Set the scene. Who, where, when, why it matters.
Build-Up (3:00–10:00): The story unfolds. Key events, decisions, turning points.
Climax (10:00–13:00): The most dramatic moment. Battle, revelation, or tragedy.
Aftermath (13:00–15:00): What happened next. Legacy. Why this story was forgotten.
CTA (15:00–15:30): "This was The Forgotten Front." Subscribe prompt. End screen.

Step 3: Voice Recording (20 min)

ActionToolTimeDetails
SetupUSB mic + Audacity2 minQuiet room. Mic 6 inches from mouth. Audacity: 44.1kHz, mono. Pop filter if available.
Record narrationAudacity12 minRead script at documentary pace (slower than conversational). Pause 2 seconds between paragraphs. Re-record any flubbed lines immediately — easier than editing later.
Basic audio cleanupAudacity6 minNoise Reduction (Effect → Noise Reduction → Get profile → Apply). Normalize volume. Compress dynamics. Export as WAV.
Audacity cheat sheet (do this every time):
1. Select a silent section → Effect → Noise Reduction → "Get Noise Profile"
2. Select All (Ctrl+A) → Effect → Noise Reduction → Apply (default settings)
3. Effect → Normalize → -1.0 dB
4. Effect → Compressor → default settings
5. Export as WAV (for editing) or MP3 (for backup)
Total cleanup: 3–5 minutes. Professional-sounding audio every time.

ActionToolTimeDetails

Step 5: Video Editing (60 min)

ActionToolTimeDetails
Import all assetsCapCut3 minImport: voiceover WAV, all images, Canva cards, background music track from YouTube Audio Library.
Lay voiceover on timelineCapCut2 minPlace full narration audio on audio track. This is your master timeline — everything syncs to voice.
Place images/footageCapCut25 minFollowing script visual cues, place images on video track. Apply Ken Burns effect (slow zoom/pan) to each image — this creates movement from still photos. Change image every 5–8 seconds.
Add text overlaysCapCut10 minNames, dates, locations as lower-thirds. Key quotes in Libre Baskerville italic. Use brand colors (amber, white on dark).
Add music trackCapCut5 minPlace background music at 10–15% volume (ducked under voice). Use suspenseful/cinematic tracks. Music should enhance mood, never overpower narration.
Add intro title card + outroCapCut5 min3-sec title card after hook. 15-sec outro with subscribe CTA and end screen placeholders.
Review and polishCapCut10 minWatch full video. Fix timing issues. Add transitions (crossfade between images, 0.5 sec). Ensure audio levels are consistent.
ExportCapCut—Export at 1080p, 30fps, H.264. File size ~500MB–1.5GB for a 15-min video.

QC

Pre-Publish Quality Checklist

Run through this before EVERY upload. Takes 5 minutes and prevents embarrassing mistakes.
CheckWhat to Verify
☐ Hook testFirst 15 seconds: does it immediately create curiosity or shock? Would YOU keep watching?
☐ Audio qualityNo background noise, consistent volume, voice clear over music. Music at 10–15% of voice volume.
☐ Visual pacingImage changes every 5–8 seconds. No static shots longer than 10 seconds. Ken Burns on all stills.
☐ Text readabilityAll text overlays readable at 720p on a phone screen. Names/dates visible for at least 3 seconds.
☐ Factual accuracyKey dates, names, and numbers double-checked against reliable sources. No AI hallucinations left in.
☐ Brand consistencyColors match palette (amber, white, dark). Fonts are Oswald/Libre Baskerville/Special Elite. Logo visible.
☐ ThumbnailReadable at 150px. Matches one of 3 templates. Bold text. Dark background. Curiosity hook.
☐ TitleUnder 60 characters. Contains primary keyword. Creates curiosity or shock. No clickbait lies.
☐ DescriptionFirst 2 lines are hook (visible before "show more"). Timestamps included. Subscribe CTA. Tags set.
☐ End screenSubscribe button + 2 recommended videos set. Cards linking to related content added.
☐ Sign-offVideo ends with: "This was The Forgotten Front. The stories they never taught you."
FIRST VIDEO

Your First Video: Step-by-Step Action Plan

Video #1: "The 2.5 Million Soldiers History Forgot" (Indian Army in WW2)
1Research (30 min): Prompt Claude for the complete story of Indian involvement in WW2. Key facts: 2.5M volunteers, 87,000 deaths, 30 Victoria Crosses, battles in Burma/Italy/North Africa. Download 20+ archival photos from IWM digital archive.
2Script (45 min): AI draft → rewrite 50%+ in your voice. Hook: "In 1939, 2.5 million men volunteered to fight for an empire that didn't consider them equal. This is the story nobody told." Structure: Why they joined → Where they fought → What they endured → Why they were forgotten.
3Record (20 min): Set up mic. Read script with documentary pacing. Pause for emphasis on emotional moments. Clean up in Audacity.
4Visuals (30 min): Create in Canva: title card, map of theaters where Indian troops served, "87,000" stat card, timeline of key battles. Collect archival photos of Indian soldiers.
5Edit (60 min): In CapCut: lay voiceover → place images with Ken Burns → add text overlays (dates, names, locations) → background music (search "somber" or "reflective" in YouTube Audio Library) → title card after hook → outro with sign-off.
6Publish (15 min): Upload. Title: "The 2.5 Million Soldiers History Forgot | Indian Army in WW2". Thumbnail: Template A "The Reveal" with red "UNTOLD" badge. Schedule for Tuesday or Thursday 3 PM EST.
7Shorts (30 min): Extract 3 Shorts: "87,000 Indian soldiers died in WW2 — and nobody remembers" (60 sec), "The Victoria Cross winners India forgot" (45 sec), "Why did 2.5 million Indians volunteer for Britain?" (60 sec). Publish over next 3 days.
Total time for Video #1 + 3 Shorts: ~4 hours. Your very first content package for The Forgotten Front. Once published, the production pipeline is proven and you repeat it with improving speed. By Video #5, you'll produce in 2.5–3 hours per long-form.
