Why YouTube SEO Matters for a New Channel

70% of YouTube views come from search + algorithm recommendations. Both are driven by metadata — your title, description, tags, and engagement signals. For a new channel with zero subscribers, SEO is your ONLY path to discovery in the first 30 days before the algorithm has enough data to recommend you organically.

TITLES

7 Proven Title Formulas for Military History

Your title is the #1 SEO signal. Primary keyword should appear in the first 40 characters. Total under 60 characters. Every title uses one of these formulas:
#FormulaExampleWhy It Works
1"The [Noun] That [Outcome]"The Battle That Saved India | Imphal 1944Creates a story in the title. The viewer must click to learn what the noun/outcome refers to.
2"Why [Number] [People] [Action]?"Why Did 80,000 Soldiers Surrender in One Day?Question format + specific number = irresistible curiosity gap. The number adds specificity and shock.
3"The [Adjective] [Noun] History [Verb]"The 2.5 Million Soldiers History ForgotPositions the story as hidden/suppressed. Viewer feels they're about to discover something forbidden.
4"[Person/Group]'s [Adjective] Secret"Churchill's Darkest Secret — The Bengal FamineNamed subject + "secret" = personal curiosity. Works for dark chapters and controversial topics.
5"How [Event] Actually [Happened]"How Fake Tanks and Sound Effects Fooled HitlerImplies the common version is wrong. Viewer clicks to learn the "real" story.
6"Why [Entity] ACTUALLY [Outcome]"Why Germany ACTUALLY Lost WW2 — It's Not What You ThinkCAPS word + "not what you think" = maximum curiosity. Use sparingly — overuse feels clickbaity.
7"The Most [Superlative] [Noun] in History"Why the World's Armies Fear These Soldiers | GurkhasSuperlatives (most feared, deadliest, largest) trigger the desire to verify. Works for list-style and profile videos.
Title rules: Under 60 characters (truncated on mobile after ~55). Primary keyword in first 40 chars. Use a pipe "|" to add context after the hook (e.g., "| Indian Army WW2"). Never ALL CAPS entire title — only ONE emphasis word if needed. Never use "Part 1" in the title of your first video on a topic (kills CTR for new viewers).

TAGS

Tag Strategy — 8-15 Tags Per Video

Tags are a secondary signal (less important than title and description) but still contribute to YouTube understanding your content. Use 8–15 tags per video following this structure:
Tag TypeCountPurposeExample (Video #1)
Exact-match primary1–2Your video's core topic exactly as viewers search it"Indian army WW2", "Indian soldiers world war 2"
Broad niche2–3Places you in the broader niche. Helps YouTube categorize your channel."military history", "world war 2", "WW2 documentary"
Long-tail specific3–5Captures niche searches with less competition. Your best chance of ranking."forgotten soldiers WW2", "Indian army Burma campaign", "British Indian army history"
Channel brand1Always include your channel name. Helps your videos show up when someone searches for you."the forgotten front"
Competitor adjacency1–2Terms that viewers of similar channels might search. NOT competitor names — that violates ToS."animated history documentary", "forgotten military stories"
Tag research shortcut: Install TubeBuddy Free → open a competitor's top video in your niche → TubeBuddy shows their tags. Use the relevant ones (don't copy blindly — only tags that genuinely match YOUR video). This takes 2 minutes and gives you proven tags.
Tags to include on EVERY video (channel-level consistency):
the forgotten front, military history, war documentary, forgotten history, untold stories, WW2, world war 2, history documentary
These 8 "evergreen" tags go on every video. Then add 5–7 topic-specific tags per video.
SHORTS

Shorts SEO — Different Rules Than Long-Form

YouTube Shorts use a different discovery system than long-form. Hashtags matter more than tags. Audio and captions are indexed. Here's how to optimize Shorts:
ElementLong-Form RuleShorts Rule
TitleUnder 60 chars, keyword-richUnder 40 chars. Hook-first. Keyword optional — Shorts are discovered more by topic than search.
Description200–400 words, keyword-rich1–3 sentences max. Include "Full story on our channel" CTA. Add 3–5 hashtags.
Tags8–15 tags3–5 tags maximum. YouTube relies more on audio and visual analysis for Shorts.
Hashtags3 max in description3–5 in description. Include #Shorts (still helps discovery), #MilitaryHistory, + 1–3 topic-specific.
CaptionsOptional (improves accessibility)MANDATORY. 85% of Shorts are watched without sound. Auto-captions in CapCut → style with bold white text.
Hook frameFirst 15 secondsFirst 1 SECOND. The opening frame is the Shorts "thumbnail." If it doesn't grab, viewer swipes away.

GOOGLE

Google Search Optimization — Videos in Google Results

23% of Google search results now display video content. Your YouTube videos can appear in Google searches too — doubling your discovery surface.
TacticHowWhy
Timestamps = Google "Key Moments"Always include timestamps in your description (formatted as "0:00 — Section Name"). Google reads these and shows "Key Moments" in search results.Google displays individual timestamps as clickable results. Your video can appear multiple times in one Google search — once for each timestamped section.
Title matches Google search queriesUse Google autocomplete (not just YouTube) to check if people search your topic on Google. If yes, match the phrasing."Why did Germany lose WW2" gets searched on Google AND YouTube. Your video can rank on both.
Description contains full contextThe algorithm paragraph in your description (the SEO paragraph near the bottom) gets indexed by Google.More text = more chances for Google to match your content to relevant queries.
Pinned comment with keywordsAfter publishing, add a pinned comment: "In this video, we explore [keyword phrase]. What forgotten military story should we cover next?"Pinned comments are indexed. The question encourages engagement (comments boost both YouTube and Google ranking).
