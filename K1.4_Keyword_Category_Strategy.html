SYSTEM

📚 How Amazon KDP Keywords Work

When you upload a book to KDP, you get 7 backend keyword slots (up to 50 characters each). These keywords, combined with your title, subtitle, and description, determine WHERE your book appears in Amazon search results.
RuleDetailsWhy It Matters
7 slots × 50 charsEach slot can be a single word or a phrase up to 50 characters including spaces.More words per slot = more search terms you're indexed for. Fill ALL 7 slots to maximum.
Don't repeat title wordsAmazon automatically indexes words in your title and subtitle. Don't waste backend slots repeating them.If your title says "Half Marathon Training Log", don't put "half marathon" in a keyword slot — use NEW terms instead.
No commas neededAmazon reads spaces as separators. "running journal fitness log" is 4 keywords, not 1 phrase.Pack multiple relevant words into each slot separated by spaces. No commas, no pipes, no separators.
No brand namesNever include competitor names, trademarked terms, or author names in keywords.Violates KDP terms of service. Can get your account suspended.
3 categoriesYou can select up to 3 Amazon store categories per book. Mix broad + mid + niche for best coverage.Categories determine which bestseller lists you can rank on. The right niche category can get you a "Bestseller" badge with just 5–10 sales/day.
Keywords unlock categoriesSome Amazon subcategories are only accessible if specific keywords exist in your metadata.Strategic keywords can place your book in additional browse paths beyond your 3 selected categories.
The 7-slot strategy (based on Kindlepreneur's 120-book experiment):
Slots 1–4: Exact-match, high-demand keyword phrases directly about your book's core topic. These are your primary discovery phrases.
Slots 5–6: Category-anchoring terms that keep your book in your chosen categories (Amazon may move your book otherwise).
Slot 7: Broad, related terms packed to 50 characters — catching wider searches you might otherwise miss.
PROCESS

🔍 Free Keyword Research Process (Repeat Per Book)

StepActionTool (Free)Time
1Amazon autocomplete mining. Go to Amazon.com → select "Books" → type your seed keyword. Write down every autocomplete suggestion. Then type "seed keyword a...", "seed keyword b...", etc. These are what real buyers search for.Amazon search bar5 min
2Competitor keyword reverse-engineering. Find the top 3–5 books in your niche. Look at their titles, subtitles, and descriptions. What words do they use repeatedly? Those are proven keywords.Amazon product pages5 min
3Category spy. On each competitor's product page, scroll down to "Product details" and note the categories they're listed under. These are your target categories.Amazon product pages3 min
4KDP Category Browser. Enter your seed keyword in this free tool to find the most relevant categories available in the KDP system.kindleranker.com (free)3 min
5Compile keyword list. From steps 1–3, you should have 30–50 potential keywords. Select the 7 most relevant and unique (not duplicating title words) for your backend slots.Google Sheets / notepad5 min
Total research time per book: ~20 minutes. For 8 books: ~2.5 hours total. Do all 8 in one session to stay in the research mindset.

TITLES

Title & Subtitle Optimization Rules

RuleDetailsExample
Title = primary keyword + hookThe main title should contain your #1 keyword phrase AND communicate what the book IS. Clear beats clever."New Dad Year One Journal" — contains "new dad" + "journal" + "year one"
Subtitle = keyword-rich benefit statementThe subtitle expands on the title with additional keywords. Include audience, format, and timeframe. Use colons or pipes to separate concepts."A First-Time Father's Memory Book & Milestone Tracker for Baby's First 12 Months"
Never keyword stuff the titleThe title must sound natural. If it reads like a list of keywords, Amazon may reject it and buyers won't trust it.BAD: "New Dad Father Baby Son Daughter Journal Notebook Planner Gift"
Include the format wordAlways include one of: Journal, Planner, Tracker, Log, Workbook, Notebook. This tells the buyer what they're getting AND captures format-based searches."E-Commerce Launch Planner" — "planner" is the format word
Add timeframe when relevant"90-Day", "16-Week", "12-Month" adds specificity and perceived value. Buyers prefer structured timeframes over open-ended products."90-Day Online Store Startup Workbook" — implies a system, not just blank pages
DESCRIPTION

Book Description — Keyword Integration Template

Your book description (4,000 characters max) should naturally incorporate keywords while selling the product. Use this template structure:
SectionContentKeywords to Include
Opening hook (2 sentences)Address the buyer's pain point or desire directly. "Finally, a [product type] designed specifically for [audience]."Primary keyword + audience keyword
What's inside (bullet points)3–5 features/sections of the book. "✓ Weekly [feature] tracker" "✓ Monthly [feature] review pages"Feature-related keywords naturally in each bullet
Who it's for (1–2 sentences)"Perfect for [audience 1], [audience 2], and [audience 3]."Audience keywords (new dads, first-time fathers, expecting parents)
Gift angle (1 sentence)"Makes an ideal gift for [occasion]." — Gift-related searches drive enormous KDP sales.Gift occasion keywords (baby shower, Father's Day, birthday, Christmas)
Specs (final line)Page count, size, paper quality. "120 pages | 6x9 inches | Premium matte cover"Format keywords (paperback, softcover, matte)
The gift hack: A huge percentage of low-content book sales are GIFTS. Including "gift for [person]" in your description (and one backend keyword slot) captures buyers searching for presents. "Gift for new dad", "gift for runner", "gift for entrepreneur" are high-volume seasonal search terms.

Keyword Refresh Schedule

WhenActionWhy
At launchUse the keywords in this document as your starting set.These are researched and ready to go.
30 days post-launchCheck KDP dashboard for impressions and clicks. If a book has low impressions, swap the weakest 2–3 keywords for alternatives from your research list.Early data reveals which keywords are actually driving discovery.
Every 90 daysFull keyword refresh. Re-run Amazon autocomplete research. Check if competitors have changed their keywords. Update 2–3 slots per book with fresh terms.Search trends change. Seasonal terms shift. New competitors enter. Keywords need maintenance.
Before seasonal peaksOctober–November: add Christmas/holiday gift keywords. May: add Father's Day keywords for dad books. January: add "new year" resolution keywords for planners.Seasonal keywords can 3–5× your sales during peak gift-buying periods.
