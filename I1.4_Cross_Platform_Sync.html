TIMING

Cross-Platform Publishing Schedule

Don't publish the same content on all platforms simultaneously. Stagger releases for maximum reach and to avoid platform penalties for duplicate content detection.
DayYouTubeInstagramTikTokTwitter/X
TuesdayLong-form Video A published (3 PM EST / 12 AM PKT+1)Reel #1 from Video A (7 PM PKT)TikTok #1 from Video A (9 PM PKT)—
WednesdayShort #1 from Video ACarousel from Video A research + StoriesTikTok #2—

FridayShort #2, Short #3Reel #3 + Stories—Thread from best video this week
Sat/Sun—Stories (behind-the-scenes, polls)——
Key timing rules:
• YouTube first, always. Your long-form video is the "source" — publish it before any derivative content hits other platforms.
• Instagram Reels: 2-4 hours after YouTube Short. Not simultaneous. Gives YouTube's algorithm time to index your Short first.
• TikTok: same day as Instagram Reel or next day. TikTok's algorithm is independent — no conflict with Instagram timing.
• Never post the exact same caption on Instagram and TikTok. Different platforms = different audiences = different captions. Reuse the video, rewrite the text.

The watermark rule is critical. If you download a video from YouTube, it has a YouTube Shorts watermark. Instagram and TikTok both reduce reach for content with competitor watermarks. ALWAYS export directly from CapCut for Instagram and TikTok. Keep the original CapCut project files organized so you can re-export any time.

When to batch: Tuesday afternoon/evening, right after uploading your YouTube video. You're already in "this week's content" headspace. The video research is fresh. The script facts are top of mind. Batching everything in one session prevents context-switching throughout the week and ensures you never forget to cross-post.
