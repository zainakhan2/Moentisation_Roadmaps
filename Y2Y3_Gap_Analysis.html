🚨 Copyright Safety Rules for Monetized Videos (CRITICAL)

The problem: A monetized YouTube channel is COMMERCIAL use. Some sources listed in Y2.2 have restrictions on commercial use. One copyright strike = channel termination risk.
SourceLicenseSafe for Monetized YouTube?Action Required
Wikimedia Commons — Public DomainPublic Domain / CC0✅ YES — fully safeCheck individual image page. If it says "Public Domain" or "CC0" → use freely. No attribution required (but good practice).
Wikimedia Commons — CC BYCreative Commons Attribution✅ YES — with attributionMust credit the creator. Add attribution in video description: "Image: [Creator name] via Wikimedia Commons, CC BY [version]".
Wikimedia Commons — CC BY-SACreative Commons Attribution Share-Alike⚠️ YES — with conditionsMust credit creator AND license your video as CC BY-SA. This means anyone could reuse YOUR video. Acceptable for YouTube but be aware of the share-alike requirement.
Wikimedia Commons — CC BY-NCNon-Commercial❌ NO — not for monetized videosNEVER use CC-NC images on a monetized channel. A monetized video is commercial use. Filter these out during research.
Library of CongressPublic Domain (US Government)✅ YES — fully safeUS government works are public domain. No restrictions. No attribution required.
National Archives (US)Public Domain (US Government)✅ YES — fully safeSame as Library of Congress. Free for all use.
Imperial War Museum (IWM)Crown Copyright (UK)⚠️ COMPLICATEDMany IWM photos are Crown Copyright — which expires 50 years after creation for UK government works. WW2 photos (1939–1945) → Crown Copyright expired 1989–1995. Most WW2-era IWM photos are now public domain. WW1 photos are definitely clear. Post-1975 photos may still be restricted. Check each image's page on IWM website for license details.
Bundesarchiv (via Wikimedia)CC BY-SA 3.0 Germany✅ YES — with attributionGermany donated 100K+ photos to Wikimedia under CC BY-SA. Credit: "Bundesarchiv, [image ID], [photographer] / CC BY-SA 3.0 DE"
Australian War MemorialCC BY-NC❌ NO — non-commercial onlyREMOVE from your safe sources list. Australian War Memorial's CC BY-NC license prohibits commercial use. Use Wikimedia Commons copies of Australian military photos instead (many have been re-licensed as Public Domain).
Pexels / PixabayPexels License / Pixabay License✅ YES — fully safeBoth allow commercial use without attribution. Safe for all purposes.
YouTube Audio LibraryRoyalty-free for YouTube✅ YES — for YouTube onlySee Gap 5 for cross-platform limitations.
REVISED SAFE SOURCE LIST (replace Y2.2 list):
🟢 ALWAYS SAFE: Wikimedia Commons (Public Domain and CC BY only), Library of Congress, National Archives (US), Pexels, Pixabay, YouTube Audio Library (YouTube only)
🟡 SAFE WITH CARE: IWM (WW2-era photos are public domain; check each post-1975 image), Wikimedia CC BY-SA (attribution + share-alike), Bundesarchiv via Wikimedia (attribution)
🔴 NEVER USE: Any CC-NC (Non-Commercial) licensed image, Australian War Memorial direct downloads, Google Image Search random results, AI-generated historical images

The 5-second check: Before downloading ANY image, look at the license line on its Wikimedia/source page. If you see "NC" or "Non-Commercial" anywhere → skip it. If you see "Public Domain", "CC0", or "CC BY" → safe to use.

GAP 3

⏰ Time Budget Reconciliation & Burnout Prevention

The problem: You said 4–6 hrs/week. We designed a 7.5+ hr/week system. With toddlers and a job, this could cause burnout in 4–6 weeks.
ScheduleLong-FormShortsPromoTotal/WeekWhen to Use
Full Week2 videos5 Shorts35 min7.5 hrsWeeks where you have time and energy. Target 2–3 per month.
Standard Week1 video5 Shorts20 min5 hrsYour DEFAULT schedule. Sustainable long-term. 1–2 per month.
Minimum Week0 videos5 Shorts (pre-batched)10 min1.5 hrsSick, travel, family emergency. Use pre-batched Shorts buffer. Max 1× per month.
Off Week0000 hrsNEVER. One week of zero uploads = algorithm penalty that takes 2–3 weeks to recover. The Minimum Week exists specifically to prevent this.
Revised realistic schedule: Target 6 long-form videos per month (not 8). Mix of Standard Weeks (1 video) and Full Weeks (2 videos). Always maintain Shorts cadence — they're faster to produce and keep the algorithm fed. At 6/month, you reach 36 long-form videos in 6 months instead of 48. This is still more than enough to hit monetization.
Burnout prevention tactics:
TacticHow
Shorts bufferOnce per month, spend 2 hours batch-producing 10 Shorts. This gives you 2 weeks of Shorts buffer for Minimum Weeks. Store them scheduled but not published.
Research batchingOne weekend per month, spend 2 hours researching + outlining 4 scripts. Then each production day, you start with a completed outline instead of from zero.
Permission to ship imperfectVideo #1 will not be as good as Video #20. That's fine. A published B+ video beats an unpublished A+ video every single time. Perfectionism is the enemy of consistency.
KDP/YouTube alternating weeksIf doing both tracks: Week 1 = YouTube focus (2 videos). Week 2 = KDP focus (book production) + 1 YouTube video. Prevents either track from stalling completely.

🛡️ Community Moderation Strategy

ScenarioExampleResponse
India-Pakistan political debate erupts"Pakistan is responsible for..." or "India is lying about..."Pin a comment: "This channel covers military HISTORY, not modern politics. Respectful historical discussion is welcome. Political attacks will be hidden." Then hide (not delete) the inflammatory comments. Deleting fuels persecution narratives.
Churchill defenders vs. critics"Churchill was a hero, this is propaganda" vs. "Churchill was a monster"Reply: "This video presents the historical record. Viewers can draw their own conclusions. I'd recommend [book title] for a deeper perspective on this debate."
Nationalist/supremacist contentComments glorifying Nazis, IJA, or any military atrocitiesHIDE immediately. Do not engage. Report if it violates YouTube's hate speech policy. Never give extremist comments visibility through engagement.
Factual corrections from experts"Actually, the troop count was 87,000 not 85,000"Heart the comment. Reply: "Thank you for the correction — you're absolutely right. I'll add a pinned note." This builds enormous credibility.
"INA: hero or traitor?" debatesPassionate disagreements about historical figuresLet the debate happen IF it stays respectful. These debates drive massive engagement signals. Only intervene if comments become personal attacks.
YouTube Studio moderation tools: Settings → Community → Automated Filters. Add blocked words: slurs, extreme political terms, spam phrases. Enable "Hold potentially inappropriate comments for review" — this catches most toxic comments before they're visible. Review held comments daily (2 minutes) during your first month.
